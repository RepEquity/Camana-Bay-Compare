    • 11 Things To Do With Kids in Grand Cayman at Camana Bay This Summertime

    26 June 2018

    Summer fun is what Camana Bay is all about. Whether you recently bought property or opened a business on Grand Cayman and are looking for ways to have fun with the whole family during the school holidays, Camana Bay has you covered! Take a look at our list of 11 ways for time to fly, and let us know if you think we are missing any key activities.

    1. Imagination Playground

    An endlessly popular attraction with children, the Imagination Playground foam shapes and blocks in the Crescent fountains allow for imaginative and constructive play while splashing around in the interactive fountains in a beautiful waterfront setting.

    1. Starfish Village drop-in

    If escaping the heat is the name of your game, try a stay and play at Starfish Village, where parents can accompany children to this educational entertainment centre for some playtime, or drop them for a supervised Drop and Play while you shop or enjoy a meal in the Town Centre.

    1. Take a trip to the other side of the island

    One of the most popular and fun attractions in the Cayman Islands is Rum Point, and it’s just a convenient 20-minute ferry crossing away with Cayman Ferries, which goes back and forth between Camana Bay and Kaibo Yacht Club on a regular schedule (its vans can then transport you quickly to Rum Point).

    1. Frozen treats

    With gelato, Häagen-Dazs, smoothies, fresh juices, milkshakes and more, the eateries of Camana Bay have a multitude of options for you to stay cool while enjoying a sweet treat. Start or finish your day the cool way!

    1. Kids Club

    With brighter lights and softer sounds, the Camana Bay Cinema Kids Club is held every Saturday at 10 a.m. and airs a family-friendly film to enchant little ones. At just CI $5 a ticket, this is a great-value activity

    1. Island fun

    If you’re looking for free things to do in Grand Cayman, spending time outdoors has to be number one. Enjoy the Island at Camana Bay, with Poof the Iguana, hammocks galore and all the sand you could want for your sandcastle adventures!

    1. Story Time

    Held every Tuesday at Camana Bay Cinema, Story Time is an interactive and free live storytelling for little ones. It fills up quickly so come early to grab a seat!

    1. Culinary camp

    Bon Vivant has a Budding Chef culinary camp for kids aged four to seven and eight to 12. With interactive lessons and take-home treats, kids will love this chance to whip up their own creations under the guidance of chef Maureen Cubbon.

    1. Keep active

    With a multi-sports summer camp and a basketball camp, Camana Bay Sports Complex will help your children burn that energy off – and the Basketball Camp will feature an NBA player as a special guest!

    1. Sunrise on the canals, sunset on the beach

    You don’t have to spend the whole day outside, but enjoy travelling between Seven Mile Beach and the canals with ease with a quick stroll over the Rise. Capture the stunning sunrise over the North Sound on the Camana Bay Harbour, and traverse the Rise to catch the sun setting over the world-famous stunning Seven Mile Beach.

    1. Snap forever memories

    Camana Bay’s Town Centre offers photo opps galore. Stop and take a snapshot with the Observation Tower’s three-million-tile mosaic, swinging on the hammocks on the Island or splashing in the Crescent fountains. If you want to print your memories up quickly, stop by Picture This Studios for their convenient photo printing service.

    Here’s a weekly at-a-glance schedule of happenings in Camana Bay:

    Mondays

    • The Observation Tower
      Walk up the majestic 75-foot Observation Tower and challenge your family to find the turtle as you explore the floor-to-ceiling underwater mosaic portraying the magnificent marine environment found in the turquoise-coloured waters surrounding Grand Cayman.
    • Camana Bay Cinema
      Cool off after a fun day at the beach and catch a film in the air-conditioned comfort of the new Camana Bay Cinema VIP theatre with leather recliners and an expanded food and beverage menu. Showtimes are available here.

    Tuesdays

    • Imagination Playground, 10 a.m.-7 p.m., The Crescent
      Your children can build their own playground in the fountains! Build in some eye-spy fun. Ask them to look around for a cool building feature or perhaps a bit of flora and fauna, and then try to replicate the design with the building blocks. There are plenty of shady areas, including rocking chairs overlooking the picturesque Camana Bay Harbour, where you can relax while your children play.  This is always a top attraction on every family’s “Cayman Islands Things to Do” list.

    Wednesdays

    • Local Harvest Market, Wednesdays, 10 a.m.-3 p.m.
      Your entire family will enjoy this lively market showcasing all that is grown and made in the Cayman Islands, plus cultural demonstrations, artists in action and live music. If Cayman Islands shopping is on your to-do list, the Camana Bay Local Harvest Market is the perfect place to buy a souvenir or a gift for someone special.
    • Open Canvas, 7 p.m.-10 p.m., KARoo
      Bring a blank canvas and paint al fresco at KARoo, a lively watering hole located on the waterfront at Camana Bay. Open Canvas welcomes artists of all ages including children, but parents do need to be present for them to participate. This session allows artists to sit and paint freely; please note, no instruction is provided. Local artists often paint here on Wednesday evenings. Bring your own supplies and canvases. Drop-ins welcome; for information or questions email J.R. at douglas.cameron@plife.ky.

     Fridays

    • Celebrate!
      Enjoy end-of-the-week treats at Gelato & Co, Petit Paris, Häagen-Dazs, Jessie’s Juice Bar and more. How many flavours of gelato can your family try this summer?
    • Explore from Sea to Sound
      After indulging in some sweet treats, walk off the calories on a sea-to-sound stroll from the Camana Bay Harbour on the North Sound all the way to Seven Mile Beach via the Rise. This 15-minute walk takes you over the new vehicular underpass and provides some great views of Grand Cayman along the route.

    Saturdays

    • Imagination Playground, 10 a.m.-7 p.m., The Crescent
      Bring your children to The Crescent where they can build their own playground in the fountains.

     Sundays

    • Island Hammocks
      Grab your favourite book or pick up a new story from Books & Books to read on an island hammock as you sway under the palm trees and enjoy the breezes off the North Sound.
    • Cayman Ferries
      Take the family on an adventure between Camana Bay, Kaibo Yacht Club and Rum Point with Cayman Ferries. Sundays are extra relaxing; however you can enjoy a round trip every Tuesday through Sunday. Reservations are required so call 1.345.325.7777 to book.
