    • February Events At Camana Bay

    01 February 2018

    Find love this February at Camana Bay.

    Camana Bay Cinema now offers Classics at the Cinema every week, and one of its showings this month fits the Valentine’s theme perfectly: When Harry Met Sally (R) on Tuesday, Feb. 6. Check out Culture at the Cinema’s live screening of Bolshoi Ballet’s version of the greatest love story ever told, Romeo and Juliet, on Feb. 17. Gnomeo & Juliet will also be showing on the Crescent as part of Valentine’s on the Big Screen. Children can get their hands into some arts and crafts before the movie begins and Camana Bay’s restaurants will have food and drinks for the ultimate outdoor dinner and a movie experience.

    February is also the month where we bring back some of our most popular events. Legends is back on the Festival Green, where tennis fans can see some of the biggest names in the game play against each other and get the opportunity to meet and greet the players. This year’s lineup includes Jimmy Connors, Pat Cash, Stefan Edberg, junior Wimbledon champion Claire Liu and current US Open champion, Sloane Stephens. Join the experts from Infinite Mindcare at Books & Books for a discussion that will improve anyone’s Valentine’s Day experience, focusing on that Crazy Little Thing Called Love: Mastering Relationships. Or take a friend or a loved one to 3 Girls and a Kiln (or go solo for some quality ‘me’ time!) for their ceramics classes, including a Zentangle Hanging Heart class on Feb. 8. And head to Camana Bay Cinema on Feb. 9, when the anticipated final installment in the Fifty Shades Grey series, Fifty Shades Freed, is released.

    To end the month, join Grammy Award-winning country music artist, Patty Griffin, for a fundraising event under the stars at Abacus, including a three-course dinner.

    Iconz Cayman presents Fantasy 2018

    Friday, Feb. 2, 5:30-10 p.m., Abacus

    Join Iconz Cayman at Abacus for a carnival happy hour and Iconz’s official costume launch for CayMAS Carnival 2018.

    Ceramics Class: Starfish Pattern Small Handled Tray

    Friday, Feb. 2, 7-9 p.m., 3 Girls and a Kiln

    Star light, star bright – learn how to glaze starfish tonight! Join Claire as she explores 3 Girls and a Kiln’s very popular starfish pattern and shows how it all comes together. The trays make the perfect gift or a beautiful addition to any home. Classes are CI$50 and include materials and light refreshments. Pre-registration is required – email 3girlsandakiln@gmail.com to sign up.

    6 Nations Rugby Games

    Saturday, Feb. 3, 9:15 a.m., The King’s Head

    6 Nations Rugby is here and The King’s Head is showing all games, starting Feb. 3 and running through Mar. 17. There is no better way to enjoy the rugby season than at The King’s Head with large screens showing all the action. Today’s matches include Wales vs. Scotland, beginning at 9:15 a.m. and France vs. England at 11:45 a.m. For all other rugby events, visit camanabay.com/events.

    Budding Chef Cooking Class: Pizza Mania

    Saturday, Feb. 3, 12-1 p.m., Bon Vivant Kitchen Studio

    Budding chefs ages four to 12 will roll up their sleeves and learn how to make a pizza from scratch with cheesy garlic knots and marinara dipping sauce. The cost is CI$35 and includes the lesson, recipes, snacks, activities and a take-home creation! Children under six must be accompanied by an adult. To register, call 345.623.2665 or email info@bonvivant.ky.

    Camana Bay Cinema’s Kids Club

    Saturdays, Feb. 3, 10, 17 & 24, 10 a.m., Camana Bay Cinema

    Kids and adults can take in classic family-friendly movies with brighter lights and softer sounds every Saturday. Tickets are CI$5 per person. For more details, visit bigscreen.ky/kids-club.

    *Feb. 3: Frozen (PG)

    *Feb. 10: Quest for Camelot (G)

    *Feb. 17: Madagascar (PG)

    *Feb 24: Puss in Boots (PG)

    *Movies are subject to change.

    Classic Movies at the Cinema

    Tuesday, Feb. 6, 7 p.m., Camana Bay Cinema

    Camana Bay Cinema features a classic movie every Tuesday as part of their Classics at the Cinema series. Shown in the VIP theatre, tickets are CI$20 per person and include a small popcorn. For more details, visit bigscreen.ky/classics.

    *Feb. 6: When Harry Met Sally (R)

    *Feb. 13: Gone with the Wind (G)

    *Feb. 20: 1941 (PG)

    *Feb. 13: Die Hard (R)

    *Movies are subject to change.

    Mexican Cantina with Chef Ervin Horvath of Agave Grill

    Wednesday, Feb. 7, 6-8 p.m., Bon Vivant Kitchen Studio

    Back by popular demand, Chef Ervin will take the class through a delicious lesson of authentic Mexican fare with an Agave Grill twist. The cost is CI$85 per person and includes food tastings and recipes. To register, call 345.623.2665 or email info@bonvivant.ky. 

    Ceramics Class: Zentangle Hanging Hearts

    Thursday, Feb. 8, 7-9 p.m., 3 Girls and a Kiln

    Perfect for the Valentine in your life! Learn the art of Zentangle with Deborah as she leads the class through simple and complex patterns. Choose from the colour schemes provided or come up with an individual combination as you decorate two ceramic hanging hearts. Classes cost CI$50, include materials and light refreshments and pre-registration is required – email 3girlsandakiln@gmail.com to sign up.

     Legends at Camana Bay

    Friday, Feb. 9, 6:30 p.m., Festival Green

    Legends at Camana Bay is returning to the Festival Green for one night only and will include an exciting line-up of men’s and women’s singles and mixed doubles with some of the biggest names in the game, including Jimmy Connors, Pat Cash, Stefan Edberg, junior Wimbledon champion Claire Liu and current US Open champion, Sloane Stephens. For more details on this event, and to purchase tickets, visit legendscayman.com.

     Ceramics Class: Beer Mug or Wine Glass

    Friday, Feb. 9, 7-9 p.m., 3 Girls and a Kiln

    Valentine’s date night or girls’ night out, this class will be a blast! Join Aimee for a fun and festive barware glazing class. Learn various glazing techniques and fun colour schemes as you decorate one ceramic beer mug or wine glass. Classes cost CI$50, include materials and light refreshments and pre-registration is required – email 3girlsandakiln@gmail.com to sign up.

     Valentine’s on the Big Screen

    Tuesday, Feb. 13, 6-9 p.m., the Crescent  

    Come out with your loved one, your friends or the entire family to the Valentine’s Day Moonlight & Movies for a family-friendly romantic movie. There will be food and drinks available for purchase before the movie starts from 6 p.m. The movie starts at 7 p.m.

    Love Bites: An Anti-Valentine’s Day Celebration

    Wednesday, Feb. 14, 6 p.m., The King’s Head

    Join The King’s Head for this year’s “anti-Valentine’s Day” celebration on Feb. 14 starting at 6 p.m. and karaoke starting at 9 p.m. Come out with your single (or paired-up!) friends to enjoy a different take on this romantic holiday.

     Date Night at Bon Vivant

    Thursday, Feb. 15, 6-8 p.m., Bon Vivant

    Continue the Valentine’s Day vibes while celebrating all things food at Bon Vivant. Served over four courses, learn how to make foolproof elegant dishes. The cost is CI$100 per person and includes food and beverage. To register or for more details, email info@bonvivant.ky.

    Budding Chef Cooking Class: Bon Vivant Burger Bash

    Saturday, Feb. 17, 12-1 p.m., Bon Vivant Kitchen Studio

    Budding chefs ages four to 12 will roll up their sleeves and learn how to make a sloppy joe sliders and mozzarella-stuffed pizza burgers. The cost is CI$35 and includes the lesson, recipes, snacks, activities and a take-home creation! Children under six must be accompanied by an adult. To register, call 345.623.2665 or email info@bonvivant.ky.

    Infinite Mindcare Talk Series

    Saturday, Feb. 17, 2-3 p.m., Books & Books

    Books & Books hosts this monthly interactive dialogue promoting awareness on different topics about mental health with the experts from Infinite Mindcare. This month’s topic is Crazy Little Thing Called Love: Mastering Relationships. For information on this event, call +1.345.640.2665.

    Culture at the Cinema

    Saturday, Feb. 17, 7 p.m., Camana Bay Cinema

    Enjoy a live screening of Bolshoi Ballet’s Romeo & Juliet in the VIP theatre. Tickets are CI$40 per person. Must be 18 years or older to attend. For more information and tickets, visit bigscreen.ky/cultureschedule.

    Ceramics Class: Latte Love and Saucer

    Saturday, Feb. 17, 7-9 p.m., 3 Girls and a Kiln

    Join Deborah as she teaches you various glazing techniques that include line designs, colour blending and image transfers on a large latte mug, the perfect surface for creativity! Classes cost CI$50, include materials and light refreshments and pre-registration is required – email 3girlsandakiln@gmail.com to sign up.

    Ceramics Class: Teen Ice Cream Bowl

    Wednesday, Feb. 21, 4-6 p.m., 3 Girls and a Kiln

    Open to teens ages 12-16. This class offers teens the chance to decorate a bowl that is not only beautiful but functional! Join Aimee for this after-school class to learn basic glazing techniques as you decorate one ice cream bowl and spoon. No experience required. Classes cost CI$50, include materials and light refreshments and pre-registration is required – email 3girlsandakiln@gmail.com to sign up.

    Ceramics Class: Marvellous Monstera Coasters (Happy Hour Event)

    Friday, Feb. 23, 5:30-7 p.m., 3 Girls and a Kiln

    Happy hour is also crafty hour! Join Claire for a festive and fun coaster class, featuring 3 Girls and a Kiln’s Marvellous Monstera pattern. This is a decorating class for a set of four ceramic coasters. Classes cost CI$50, include materials and light refreshments and pre-registration is required – email 3girlsandakiln@gmail.com to sign up.

     An Evening with Patty Griffin under the Stars

    Friday, Feb. 23, 6-10 p.m., Abacus

    This fundraising dinner features a live performance from Grammy Award-winning country music artist, Patty Griffin. For more details, visit stay-focused.org/patty-griffin-grand-cayman-2018/.

    Budding Chef Cooking Class: Pasta 101

    Saturday, Feb. 24, 12-1 p.m., Bon Vivant Kitchen Studio

    Budding chefs ages four to 12 will roll up their sleeves and learn how to make a bowtie pasta from scratch and lasagna rolls. The cost is CI$35 and includes the lesson, recipes, snacks, activities and a take-home creation! Children under six must be accompanied by an adult. To register, call 345.623.2665 or email info@bonvivant.ky.

     Lovin’ Local

    Wednesday, Feb. 28, 6-8 p.m., Bon Vivant Kitchen Studio

    Focus on local and Caribbean foods in this class featuring goodies such as conch fritters, fresh catch ceviche and a step-by-step lesson on how to make classics with a twist like Cayman-style beef tacos! The cost is CI$75 per person and includes food tastings. To register, call 345.623.2665 or email info@bonvivant.ky.

    Ceramics Class: High-Heel Wine Bottle Holder

    Wednesday, Feb. 28, 7-9 p.m., 3 Girls and a Kiln

    Shoe love is true love! Join Aimee to decorate a ceramic shoe that also doubles as a wine bottle holder. Choose from a variety of stencils and colours. Classes cost CI$50, include materials and light refreshments and pre-registration is required – email 3girlsandakiln@gmail.com to sign up.

    WEEKLY RITUALS

    Virtual Reality Games at The King’s Head

    Mondays, 5:30 p.m., The King’s Head
    Every Monday, step into a virtual reality universe as The King’s Head hosts RentVR, starting at 5:30 p.m. Come and experience the latest and best in computer gaming.

     Toddler Tuesdays
    Tuesdays: Imagination Playground 10 a.m.-7 p.m., the Crescent; and Camana Bay Story Time 11-11:30 a.m., Camana Bay Cinema

    Children can enjoy splashing in the interactive fountains at Imagination Playground and take part in a live story reading and sing along with exciting new books and beloved children’s songs with Camana Bay Story Time. These events are free and open to the public.

    Farmers & Artisans Market

    Wednesdays, 12-7 p.m., the Paseo
    Discover an array of local purveyors offering products and experiences found only in Cayman. Come early for the widest selection of fresh produce and farm goods then browse an eclectic selection of artisanal products and handicrafts.

     Open Canvas

    Wednesdays, 7 p.m., KARoo

    Every Wednesday, Open Canvas provides a public venue for local artists to display their talents and work on their latest creations. Art enthusiasts can join the gathering and watch paintings come to life one brushstroke at a time. Artists can contact douglascameron@cib.ky to reserve an easel and must bring their own art supplies. For more details, call +1.345.946.6633.

     The Flavour Tour

    Wednesdays, 7-9 p.m., West Indies Wine Company

    Experience the new format of the Flavour Tour! Whet your appetite with a sommelier-led tasting of two wines at West Indies Wine Company, then experience Camana Bay’s dining scene as your host takes you on a guided culinary adventure at four Grand Cayman restaurants for a tantalising meal. The evening will wrap up with a digestif offering at KARoo. The cost is US$89 per person all-inclusive. To purchase tickets, visit camanabay.com/flavour-tour

     

    Friday Night Live Music
    Fridays, 7-10 p.m., Abacus

    Enjoy contemporary Caribbean sounds at Abacus with live entertainment on Friday evenings. Stop by for drinks before catching a movie or stay for dinner and welcome your weekend in style. For more details, call +1.345.623.8282.

    Imagination Playground

    Saturdays, 10 a.m.-7 p.m., the Crescent

    Imagination Playground invites kids to build, dream and explore a new world every week in the Town Centre’s dancing fountains with giant foam blocks in all shapes and sizes. Little ones can recreate familiar objects like animals, rocket ships and more. This event is every Tuesday and Saturday and is free and open to the public.

     Story & Craft Time
    Saturdays, 10:30 a.m., Books & Books

    Young readers and their parents can join the storytellers at Books & Books for exciting new tales and crafts that inspire imagination and creativity.

    Saturday Night Buffet with Live Music
    Saturdays, 7-10 p.m., Abacus

    Enjoy Caribbean-inspired jazz performed live from 7-10 p.m. as you indulge in a buffet featuring some of Abacus’ chefs’ best creations, including options from the land and sea. For more details, call +1.345.623.8282.

    Sunday BBQ Brunch
    Sundays, 11 a.m.-3 p.m., KARoo
    Savour a tasty selection of fresh off-the-grill favourites with all the traditional BBQ fixings for CI$30 per person, and CI$12.50 for children ages 12 and under. Enjoy 20% off all drinks and “all-you-can-bubble” for CI$19. For more details, call +1.345.946.6633.

     Dim Sum Brunch
    Sundays, 11:30 a.m.-3 p.m., Mizu Asian Bistro + Bar
    Enjoy an expanded selection of dim sum and more at Mizu Asian Bistro + Bar’s Dim Sum Brunch every Sunday. Indulge in a selection of appetisers, soups and sushi with an array of noodle, rice and main dishes. The bar includes bubbles, house wine, domestic bottles of beer and mixed well drinks. The cost is CI$49 per person all-inclusive and reservations are required at +1.345.640.0001.
