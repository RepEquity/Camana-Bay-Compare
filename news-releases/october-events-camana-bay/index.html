    • October Events at Camana Bay

    04 October 2018

    This October, Camana Bay is hosting activities and events to celebrate and encourage positive health, both mental and physical, at every age. Start off the month by showing your support for a good cause with the Anti-Bullying Fair, presented by the Family Resource Centre and Hurley’s Media on Saturday, Oct. 6. Children will be encouraged to showcase their creative work to raise awareness of the social effects of bullying in school and all its forms. Families are also encouraged to stay fit for a cause by taking part in the Mental Health Walk and Talk, presented by the Alex Panton Foundation on Wednesday, Oct. 10, and the Save Our Youth (SOY) Monster Dash on Saturday, Oct. 20. October is marked globally as Breast Cancer Awareness Month, and everyone is invited to participate in the annual Pilates for Pink, run by ENERGY Essential Fitness.

    Shoppers’ Night Out is back this year, happening on Thursday, Oct. 18 with a night of special offers and promotions for participating shops and restaurants in Camana Bay. Shopper will enjoy music, food, refreshments and chances to win raffle prizes all night long! Late-Night Shopping at Camana Bay will kick off the same night, with shops staying open until 8 p.m. on Thursdays from Oct. 18 until the holiday season ends, making it easier than ever to sneak in those after-work errands and pick up those gifts and supplies for the weekend.

    We can’t leave the month of October without a Halloween-themed family event. On Wednesday, Oct. 31, Camana Bay will host Halloween on the Big Screen on the Crescent. This year the public will have the opportunity to vote, through Camana Bay’s social media, to choose which film will be shown that night!

    Classics at the Cinema

    Tuesdays throughout October, 7 p.m., Camana Bay Cinema

    Camana Bay Cinema features a classic movie every Tuesday as part of its Classics at the Cinema series. Shown in the VIP theatre, tickets are CI$20 per person and include a small popcorn. For more details, visit bigscreen.ky/classics.

    *October 2: The Great Gatsby (1974, PG-13)

    *October 9: Ghostbusters (1984, PG)

    *October 16: Batman the Movie (1966, PG-13)

    *October 23: Drunken Master (PG-13)

    *October 30: The Exorcist (R)

    *Movies are subject to change.

     Camana Bay Cinema’s Kids Club

    Saturdays throughout October, 10 a.m., Camana Bay Cinema

    Kids and adults can take in classic family-friendly movies with brighter lights and softer sounds every Saturday. Tickets are CI$5 per person. For more details, visit bigscreen.ky/kids-club.

    *October 6: Rise of the Guardians (PG)

    *October 13: Frozen (PG)

    *October 20: Kung Fu Panda (PG)

    *October 27: The Addams Family (PG-13)

     *Movies are subject to change.

     Anti-Bullying Fair presented by The Family Resource Centre and Hurley’s Media

    Saturday, October 6, 9:30 a.m.-1 p.m.

    Arts & Recreation Centre

    The Family Resource Centre will host the Stood Up Fair which aims to present a united front against bullying in all its forms. Children in two age brackets – ages 10 to 12 and 13 to 16 – will have the chance to showcase their creativity and ingenuity in bringing awareness to the social effects of bullying in school and beyond. Participants are encouraged to use a wide variety of media to convey their message, including but not limited to posters, display boards, sculptures, paintings, PowerPoint presentations, poems and photography.

    Story Time with Avagaye Clarke-Heron

    Saturday, October 6, 10:30 a.m.

    Books & Books

    Children ages four to seven can join local author, Avagaye Clarke-Heron, as she reads from her book, Cock-A-Doodle-Choo! Mr. Rooster gets the Flu. This event is free and open to the public.

    Mental Health Walk and Talks presented by the Alex Panton Foundation

    Wednesday, October 10, 5-7 p.m.

    Town Centre

    Take part in this evening walk/run through Camana Bay in an effort to promote mental health. Proceeds to benefit the Alex Panton Foundation.

    The Bookends Club

    Join The Bookends Club as they meet to review their latest pick, I Know Why the Caged Bird Sings by Maya Angelou. Come share your insights and impressions in an open discussion. The Bookends Club gathers on the second Wednesday of every month and new members are always welcome.

     Pilates for Pink

    Saturday, October 13, 9-11 a.m., Arts & Recreation Centre

    ENERGY Essential Fitness hosts a fitness class to raise funds for breast cancer awareness. Bring a mat and wear pink to join a fun workout where prizes are awarded throughout the class. Registration fee is CI$30 per person. All proceeds benefit the Cayman Islands Cancer Society. For details and to register call +1.345.946.6006 or email info@energycayman.com.

    Shoppers’ Night Out

    Thursday, October 18, 5-8 p.m., Town Centre

    Come out to Camana Bay for a night of special offers and promotions from our shops and restaurants, along with the chance to win raffle prizes while enjoying music, food and refreshments! This special evening will launch Thursday Late-Night Shopping at Camana Bay, where participating shops will stay open late every Thursday from October 18 until the end of the holiday season!

    SOY Monster Dash

    Saturday, October 20, 6-8 a.m., Town Centre

    Take part in the 4th Annual 5K walk/run to benefit the Save Our Youth (SOY) Foundation. All proceeds will benefit the organisation’s programmes and activities.

    Infinite Mindcare Talk Series

    Saturday, October 20, 2-3 p.m., Books & Books

    Books & Books hosts this monthly interactive dialogue promoting awareness on different topics about mental health with the experts from Infinite Mindcare. This month’s topic is Resilience: Learning to Roll with the Punches. For information on this event, call +1.345.640.2665.

     Culture at the Cinema

    Saturday, October 20, 8 p.m., Camana Bay Cinema

    Enjoy a pre-recorded screening of Frankenstein in the VIP theatre. Tickets are CI$40 per person. Must be 18 years or older to attend. For more information and tickets, visit bigscreen.ky/cultureschedule.

    Lustic Life Book Club

    Thursday, October 25, 6-7:30 p.m., West Indies Wine Company

    Sophie Prior of Lustic Life invites you to the Lustic Life Book Club, happening every month at West Indies Wine Company. Each month’s book choice will be available for purchase at a discount of 20% off at Books & Books. This month’s book is The Energy Bus by Jon Gordon. This interactive class encourages readers to jot down their favourite quotes and passages and bring them to the discussion. To register, email sophie@lusticlife.com.

    Halloween on the Big Screen

    Wednesday, October 31, 7 p.m., the Crescent

    Enjoy a family-friendly Halloween-themed flick on the big screen on the Crescent! The public can take part in a social media movie poll from Oct. 1-15 and choose which film will be shown on the night!

     WEEKLY RITUALS

    Toddler Tuesdays
    Tuesdays: Imagination Playground 10 a.m.-7 p.m., the Crescent; and Camana Bay Story Time 11-11:30 a.m., Camana Bay Cinema

    Children can enjoy splashing in the interactive fountains at Imagination Playground and take part in Halloween-themed interactive story readings throughout the month of October with Camana Bay Story Time. These events are free and open to the public.

     Local Harvest Market

    Wednesdays in Heliconia Court and Saturdays opposite Jessie’s Juice Bar throughout October, 10 a.m.-3 p.m.
    From mid-August to mid-November, come out and shop for fresh and local produce from local farmers every Wednesday in Heliconia Court, located next to Cayman Dental and across from Petit Paris, and every Saturday opposite Jessie’s Juice Bar.

     Open Canvas

    Wednesdays, 7 p.m., KARoo

    Every Wednesday, Open Canvas provides a public venue for local artists to display their talents and work on their latest creations. Art enthusiasts can join the gathering and watch paintings come to life one brushstroke at a time. Artists can contact douglascameron@cib.ky to reserve an easel and must bring their own art supplies. For more details, call +1.345.946.6633.

     The Flavour Tour

    Wednesdays, 7-9 p.m., West Indies Wine Company

    Savour the flavours of island life on the Camana Bay Flavour Tour! With 3 plates at Camana Bay’s restaurants, 4 beverages and 5 stops, this guided walking tour highlights local ingredients and offers a fun and diverse dining event not to be missed. The menu changes every week and is crafted the day of the tour, with fresh ingredients from the morning’s market. The cost is US$89 per person all-inclusive. To purchase tickets, visit camanabay.com/flavourtour. To enquire about private group bookings, please email flavourtour@camanabay.com for additional information.

    Friday Night Live Music
    Fridays, 7-10 p.m., Abacus

    Enjoy contemporary Caribbean sounds at Abacus with live entertainment on Friday evenings. Stop by for drinks before catching a movie or stay for dinner and welcome your weekend in style. For more details, call +1.345.623.8282.

    Imagination Playground

    Saturdays, 10 a.m.-7 p.m., the Crescent

    Imagination Playground invites kids to build, dream and explore a new world every week in the Town Centre’s dancing fountains with giant foam blocks in all shapes and sizes. Little ones can recreate familiar objects like animals, rocket ships and more. This event is every Tuesday and Saturday and is free and open to the public.

    Sing Along Story Time
    Saturdays, 10:30 a.m., Books & Books

    Young readers four to seven years old and their parents can join the storytellers at Books & Books to sing along to some of their favourite books.

     Saturday Night Live Music
    Saturdays, 7-10 p.m., Abacus

    Enjoy relaxing tunes performed live from 7-10 p.m. as you indulge in a tapas menu and drinks or treat yourself to a dinner date. For more details, call +1.345.623.8282.

    Sunday BBQ Brunch
    Sundays, 11 a.m.-3 p.m., KARoo
    Savour a tasty selection of fresh off-the-grill favourites with all the traditional BBQ fixings for CI$30 per person and CI$12.50 for children ages 12 and under. For more details, call +1.345.946.6633.

     Dim Sum Brunch
    Sundays, 11:30 a.m.-3 p.m., Mizu Asian Bistro + Bar
    Enjoy an expanded selection of dim sum and more at Mizu Asian Bistro + Bar’s Dim Sum Brunch every Sunday. Indulge in a selection of appetisers, soups and sushi with an array of noodle, rice and main dishes. The cost is CI$49 per person all-inclusive and reservations are required at +1.345.640.0001.

    –END–

    For full event details and more Town Centre happenings, download the Camana Bay Events App available on the App Store and Google Play or visit camanabay.com/events.
