    Amateur Cooking With Passion

    Ian Dawson-James loves to cook at home.

    Life and Culture

    June 2017
    By Lisa Boushy

    Life and Culture

    June 2017 - Lisa Boushy

    For some people, cooking is its own reward. This is true for four amateur chefs, all of whom work at Camana Bay and have in common one key ingredient: passion.

    For Ian Dawson-James, co-owner along with his wife, Catherine, of Sand Angels, Little Angels and Activ Angels in Camana Bay, food has been a passion since kindergarten, when he loved to bake cookies and cakes.

    He grew up surrounded by the sights and smells of food while living in hotels, thanks to his family’s hotelier business. Still, nothing compared to his grandmother’s home cooking. “She was an amazing cook and I remember vividly how she would pick vegetables in her garden, get meat directly from the butcher and milk straight from the dairy farm; everything was fresh and natural,” says Ian, adding, “I guess this was my first exposure to farm-to-table cooking — simple, honest, and delicious.”

    Ian started to cook for himself in his early teens and later developed a desire to experiment and try new things.

    He also has a sweet tooth and won top prize for a dessert he created for the very first Cayman Cookout cook-off held in 2009 at The Ritz-Carlton, Grand Cayman, where he was judged by world-famous chefs, including Eric Ripert.

    Although Ian doesn’t have a signature dish, he has finally perfected his steak and burger recipes, after years of experimenting. “The perfect steak for me has a slightly crispy well-seasoned crust and a rich umami taste that reveals a mouthwateringly juicy, warm, tender pink centre,” he says.

    His burgers have a meaty caramelised flavour, which he likes to serve with potatoes or a lightly toasted brioche bun, melted cheese and fresh condiments. “The French fries are always blonde and salted perfectly.”

    Although he doesn’t rule out opening a restaurant someday, Ian says nothing beats cooking in his own kitchen. “Having time to sit with close friends and eat, drink and talk about life and everything else — I deeply treasure these moments.”

    A FAMILY PASSION

    Both Morna Chisholm and her son Alejandro work at Mourant Ozannes during the day; at night and on weekends, though, they pursue their passion for cooking by whipping up dishes for friends and family.

    Morna’s mother and aunt taught her how to cook while growing up; she’s now passed on her skills to her sons (Armando, too), so that they can take care of themselves. “Plus, I like to eat a lot, so I had to learn,” says Alejandro, who loves cooking pasta dishes.

    The mother-son duo has a playful relationship in the kitchen. “Alejandro thinks it’s a playing field like his football games,” she says, but Alejandro has a different take: “I like to joke around which sometimes agitates my mom, but hey, what’s the fun of working with a partner if you can’t enjoy it? She knows it’s all love.”

    While Morna gets inspiration from new recipes and special family celebrations, Alejandro likes tweaking a recipe to his own specifications. “Most of my creations have two inspirations: my stomach and my tongue,” he says.

    Morna is an avid baker and her Spanish dessert “MoChi’s Tres Leche with Four Milks” is a hit with friends and family, while Alejandro’s “Latino Lasagna” is his signature dish.
    Together, they have catered several Mourant office events, private parties held by fellow employees, children’s birthday parties and family gatherings.

    Through it all, they have received great reviews and dream of opening up their own restaurant someday. Alejandro has even drawn up plans for it and hopes it will be a hit.

    LEARNING BY DOING

    From a very young age, Regina Robinson-Gooding had a passion for cooking; and apart from taking home economics classes in school, she has no formal training. She did learn a few things from her mother and has always had a determined nature. “I couldn’t wait until my mother left the house to start my experiments and surprise her with dinner when she returned from work.”

    Regina works at State Street Cayman Trust Company, but on her days off and weekends, she loves cooking up food and then inviting her hungry friends over. All she asks is that they bring wine. “I love cooking anything and everything — even if I don’t eat it, I can cook it. I just love to see people eating and enjoying good food.”

    Her friends often enlist her to cater their house parties and barbecues on the weekends, and her family usually requests she does the cooking for their family events.

    Although Regina has many dishes in her repertoire, like her “Apple Cake Without Eggs” dessert, her signature dish is “Oven-Baked Jerk Ribs.”

    She would like to open up a 24-hour diner one day and has already picked out the name— 3D Diner — which is a loving nod to her three children. “My kids’ names all start with the letter D.”

    Ian Dawson-James’ “Mango Magic!”

    INGREDIENTS
    2 fresh local ripe mangoes
    2/3 cup turbinado (brown) sugar
    3 ounces butter
    1/3 cup dark rum
    1 vanilla bean or a few drops of pure vanilla extract
    Curry powder (yellow)

    INSTRUCTIONS
    Prepare the fruit. Use the whole pieces
    of peeled ripe local mangoes, cut into
    small cubes.
    Melt butter and sprinkle sugar in the bottom of the hot pan on medium-hot heat. Mix until all sugar in the pan begins to caramelise.
    Add the the fresh vanilla bean (cut in half and scraped with the back of the knife) or a few drops of vanilla extract to the caramelised sugar. Make sure not to overheat it.
    Add the fruit and mix together to coat.
    Toss only once or twice to avoid breaking
    up the fruit.
    Set the heat to medium.
    Let simmer for a couple of minutes.
    Add the dark rum then put the pan back near the flame and safely flambé or just mix in.
    Add a good three-finger pinch of curry powder and mix into fruit and gently stir for a minute to combine.
    Remove from heat and serve immediately.
    Serve with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or whipped fresh cream on top.

    Regina Robinson -Gooding’s “Apple Cake without Eggs”

    INGREDIENTS
    Apple preparation:
    2 Granny Smith apples (peeled and thinly sliced)1 stick of butter
    1 cup of brown sugar
    1 tablespoon of all-purpose flour
    1/4 cup of water

    Cake batter:
    1 cup of milk
    1 stick of butter
    1 cup of self-rising flour
    1 cup of sugar (white)
    1 drop of vanilla extract

    INSTRUCTIONS
    In a saucepan combine apples with brown sugar and butter together and sauté. Once the apples soften, mix a tablespoon of flour with water then combine with cooked apples to thicken the mixture.

    Combine all of the batter ingredients except the butter in a large bowl. Melt the butter and pour in a baking dish. Add the batter mixture and then spoon in your apples and bake at 325 degrees for about 45 minutes. Serve warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.
