    Planning The Perfect Valentine’s Day Camana Bay makes it easy

    This bouquet of 50 red roses, part of Belle by Celebrations' 50 Shades of Love collection, is a $599 showstopper, but smaller Valentine's Day bouquets start around $75.

    Life and Culture

    February 2017
    By Alan Markoff

    Life and Culture

    February 2017 - Alan Markoff

    No matter the stage of your relationship with someone, Valentine’s Day can give you a good reason to be romantic.

    Women typically know how to plan for Valentine’s Day almost instinctively, but for men, it’s usually not so easy, partially because their planning for Valentine’s Day often starts on 13 February.

    “Some men come in here slightly panicked,” says Alyssa Solomon of Camana Bay’s Le Visage, a luxury cosmetics and skincare boutique. “We help them.”

    If patrons have bought products from Le Visage before, Alyssa says they’ve taken their name and recorded what they’ve bought. Based on that, it’s easier to make a gift selection. Some women also proactively create a “wish list” at Le Visage just in case their men come searching for a gift. Even if a woman has never shopped at Le Visage before, Alyssa says she can help a man choose something that any woman would be happy to receive as a gift for Valentine’s Day.

    “We try to make it as easy as possible,” Alyssa says.

    That’s the key word: easy. You make shopping easy for a man and you’ve got a customer. And easy is planning Valentine’s Day at Camana Bay.

    FLOWERS AND CLOTHES

    If you’re looking for a tried-and-true Valentine’s Day gift, flowers are a good way to go. Camana Bay’s Belle by Celebrations makes it very easy to buy flowers: Just step up to the Flower Bar, look through Belle’s offerings, write down what you want to the accompanying card to say, then pay and away you go. If you’re at a loss as to what to get, sales representative Chin Levy will help you.

    “Guys come in here and they don’t know what they want, so I help them,” she says.

    Ah … there’s a pattern developing: Apparently most men are clueless when it comes to planning for Valentine’s Day, but the people working in the shops realise that and expect it, so they’re there to help.

    Chin says red roses are the most popular choice for Valentine’s Day, but mixed bouquets that include some red roses are also very popular.

    Clothes are another popular gift for Valentine’s Day and the designer boutique NKY sells a lot of men’s shirts as presents. The women generally know what style, brand and colours the men in their life like, as well as their size. Right next door, at NKY Collections, the women’s designer boutique, there’s a familiar refrain: It seems most men don’t even know their valentine’s clothes size.

    “They’ll say something like, ‘she’s about your size,’” says NKY Collections sales representative, Emily Mills.

    But just as it is at the other Camana Bay shops, the staff are there to help whether you’re looking for resort wear, an evening gown, a cocktail dress or a sexy romper for your valentine.

    Another popular Valentine’s Day gift is lingerie and Silhouette Boutique owner Roberta Bodden says it’s a gift that makes both the giver and the recipient happy.

    “It’s a gift for her, but he can enjoy it too,” she says, adding that Silhouette also sells sleepwear that every woman would love.

    Apparently most men are clueless when it comes to planning for Valentine’s Day.

    DECADENT OPTIONS

    Pastries, chocolates and other sweets are also gifts generally happily received by your sweetie on Valentine’s Day. Bay Market has a good selection of gourmet organic chocolates, including some special Valentine’s Day chocolates. Petit Paris French bakery will have Valentine’s Day-themed cakes and pastries, and they also sell an assortment of imported French candies, some like les Anis de Flavigny bonbons that come in small decorative tins.

    Jewellery is always a great option for Valentine’s Day, and if you can afford diamonds, then Island Jewellers is the place to go at Camana Bay. It’s the place to go even if you can’t afford diamonds because they also have special Valentine’s Day charms and gift sets that start at US$65.

    Those looking for something less traditional can buy books about love or love poetry at Books & Books or a gift certificate for spa services at Eclipze Hair Design & Day Spa that will pamper the special person in your life with a manicure, pedicure or massage.

    Another unique Valentine’s Day gift would be a photography session at Picture This Studios. Everyone thinks of having couples photos done at weddings, but having professional photographs done periodically through a marriage is a romantic gesture that is bound to tug on heartstrings.

    PLANNING THE EVENING

    Gifts are one thing, but you’re still going to have to figure out what to do on Valentine’s Day evening. There’s nothing wrong with staying home and having a romantic dinner (if you don’t have kids there), and if you go that route, consider visiting West Indies Wine Company first. Rosé wines and Champagnes are great choices for a romantic meal at home, but a good red wine such as Amarone or Cabernet Sauvignon is an excellent option as well.

    Camana Bay also offers several options for a romantic evening out, starting with cocktails a KARoo followed by dinner for two at Mizu, Abacus or Lola Bistro, and finishing up with cool treats at Häagen-Dazs or Gelato & Co. You can also catch a movie at Regal Camana Bay Stadium 6, which will feature “Fifty Shades Darker,” the second in the erotic romance film series.

    Belle by Celebrations has even named this year’s Valentine’s Day flower connection “Fifty Shades of Love” to complement the release of the film. The signature piece of the collection is a colossal 50-rose bouquet.

    “It’s a real show-stopper,” said the shop’s owner, Jo Anne Brown. “The arrangements have all been artfully crafted to reflect the romance of the story, incorporating lace, pearls and other flirty nods to the film in their design.”

    All of what the shops, restaurants and eateries offer can help you plan a perfect Valentine’s Day, but not everything will cost you money at Camana Bay. A stroll along the Crescent’s boardwalk, holding hands with your loved one, and maybe sharing a kiss in a dark, secluded corner can create a lasting romantic memory that costs nothing, but is sentimentally priceless.
