    The Dream Turns 10

    Dart News

    November 2017
    By Alan Markoff

    Dart News

    November 2017 - Alan Markoff

    It started as a vision, a dream, of one man: Ken Dart. The dream led to a mantra, written more than a decade ago by the creative agency Slaughter. That mantra, woven through this writing, speaks of a place where life blossoms.

    We create our town like we create a garden. Something that will last.

    Following the purchase of the former Coral Caymanian hotel and 238 acres of land stretching from Seven Mile Beach to the North Sound,
    Dart started planning a community unlike anything previously seen in the Cayman Islands. He established a nursery for plants and trees soon after he purchased the property. Because of his foresight, Camana Bay’s gardens now feature 250 different species and more than 40,000 individual plants. A new standard in landscaping evolved on Grand Cayman.

    Beyond today. And bear fruit for generation after generation. This is Camana Bay.

    It took nearly a decade of meticulous planning with some of the world’s best architects and master planners before construction on buildings commenced. The master plan had to reflect the vision, but it also had to reflect its location.

    For Cayman. Of Cayman. Camana Bay.

    Built in the years after Hurricane Ivan, Camana Bay was part of the new Grand Cayman: wiser, stronger, more resilient, but also adaptable to change. Plans changed, roads moved, more land was acquired. Camana Bay has grown to 675 acres.

    A special place, living and growing and renewing itself season after season.

    Construction started in 2005. Cayman International School welcomed its first students in 2006. The Town Centre’s first building opened its doors in November 2007, welcoming EY, Cayman National and London & Amsterdam Trust Company. In the months and years that followed, the town became home for a myriad of businesses — places to shop, places to dine, places to work and places to enjoy life. The first tenants moved into the The Terraces in 2009. Camana Bay became a lifestyle; a place to live, work, learn and play.

    This is Camana Bay. A place where life is embraced. Nature. Architecture. Fashion. Food. Entertainment. A place cultivated with care. A place cultivated with the seeds of Caymanian tradition and culture.

    The weekly Farmers & Artisans Market celebrates products “Made in Cayman.” Iconic Cayman Islands’ catboats have raced in the Harbour. The works of local filmmakers have premiered at the cinema. Countless fundraisers hosted in the Town Centre have supported local charities. And all of this embedded in a landscape of native flora: sea grape trees, royal palms, silver thatch, sea lavender … and so many more species.

    A place cultivated with the seeds of Caymanian tradition and culture.

    Since its opening, Camana Bay has hosted thousands of Grand Cayman’s best events: big events, small events, important events, fun events. The annual Tree Lighting has become Grand Cayman’s most beloved Christmas-season ritual. Taste of Cayman is a highlight of the annual culinary calendar. Top international chefs have cooked here. Famous authors have read from their works here. Winemakers from all over the world have led tastings here. Some sporting events have featured internationally known athletes, while local competitions have promoted wellness and enriched the lives of residents.

    And a place nurtured with the best of what the world has to offer.

    Some people call Camana Bay home. More than 1,700 people work here, including some of the leading professionals in their fields. Tens of thousands of residents and tourists visit each month, enjoying what the Town Centre offers, including its hallmark walkability.

    This is Camana Bay. A place where life flourishes. A place where life unfolds.

    People get engaged at Camana Bay. Some get married here. The lush, tropical landscaping provides a backdrop for wedding photographs. Residents celebrate milestones at Camana Bay: a promotion; an anniversary; a graduation; a birthday. Children and families visit Town Centre, forging lasting memories.

    A place where life is vibrant. A place where life thrives.

    Now we celebrate Camana Bay. The Town Centre opened a decade ago. On November 9, the Dart group of companies will throw a party — an anniversary party, a birthday party — for Camana Bay. Everyone is invited.

    This is Camana Bay. For Cayman. Of Cayman.

    It started as a vision, a dream, of one man: Ken Dart. The realisation of that dream now turns 10 years old. Happy Birthday, Camana Bay. But the story doesn’t end here. There’s much more to come. Camana Bay’s next 10 years promise to be just as dynamic, just as innovative, just as impactful for the Cayman Islands as the first 10 years.
    Camana Bay is …

    A place where life blossoms.
