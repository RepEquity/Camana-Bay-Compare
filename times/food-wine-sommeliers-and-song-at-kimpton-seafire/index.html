    Food, wine, sommeliers and song at Kimpton Seafire

    Toots and the Maytals played a free concert the night of the wine dinner.

    Dart News

    February 2017
    By Alan Markoff

    Dart News

    February 2017 - Alan Markoff

    It was an evening of firsts on 14 January when food, wine and music converged at Kimpton Seafire Resort + Spa.

    With Grand Cayman immersed in the Cayman Cookout food and wine festival that weekend, Seafire’s Avecita restaurant hosted one of the festival’s Saturday night “Dine Around” events for the first time.

    Billed as “First Taste,” the dinner event featured the world launch of Lingua Franca wines, a collaboration between two legendary wine personalities, French winemaker Dominique Lafon and renowned American master sommelier Larry Stone. In addition to Lafon and Stone, other notable wine industry people in attendance were Lingua Franca winemaker Thomas Savre and well-known sommelier Rajat Parr, who was mentored by Stone and has become a noted wine producer in his own right.

    “Launching our new wines at Kimpton Seafire seemed like the perfect fit,” said Stone, who commented on the fact that he had previous working relationships with two of the Avecita staff, sommelier Joshua Wludyka and restaurant general manager Andrew Bradbury, who was able to put together the event with a text message.

    “We were in a meeting talking about what to do for the Cayman Cookout and I knew Larry was going to launch his new wine around this time, so I sent him a text and asked if he’d want to do it here. Literally 45 seconds later, he called and said he would do it.”

    Getting Lafon on board after that was easy, Bradbury said.

    “It’s freezing this time of the year [in Burgundy] and we were offering the sunny Caribbean.”

    Stone said that coming to Seafire and the Cayman Islands for the first time ever, with his friends Joshua and Andrew presiding over the event, seemed like a homecoming for him.

    “I can’t imagine a better place to make our debut.”

    Following a welcome reception with passed canapés, guests enjoyed a four-course dinner created under the guidance of Kimpton chefs Massimo Defrancesca, George Marrone and Remy Lefebvre.

    Each of the savoury dishes was paired with Chardonnay or Pinot Noir wines made at the Lingua Franca winery in Oregon’s Willamette Valley. Included in the wine selections was “Joshua, Junichi and Siri” Pinot Noir, partially named after Wludyka, who worked at the winery over the past summer, helping to produce the wine. That wine was paired with the highlight dish of the evening, “Hen & Truffle Cannelloni” with manchego cheese, smoked lobster, saffron broth and shaved black truffle.

    When the dinner was over, guests were invited to attend a concert featuring the award-winning Jamaican reggae band, Toots and the Maytals, who performed a selection of their #1 hit songs on the lawn.

    Bradbury said that Avecita plans to have wine dinners featuring some of the biggest names in the industry on a quarterly basis.

    “This event was just setting the stage for something much bigger,” he said. “There will be surprises. There will be events where people in New York will raise their eyebrows and say, ‘They’re bringing who, where?’”
